This document is intended to be an easy-to-read reference the currently recommended terms of the Mineralogy Extension. This document is not part of the standard. It draws on the term names and definitions from the normative part of the standard and combines them with comments and examples that are not normative, but that are meant to help people to use the terms consistently. Comprehensive metadata for current and obsolete terms in human readable form are found in a list of terms document. List of files and types can be found in the Mineralogy Extension repository.

Chemistry

geoext:Chemistry geoext:measuredChemistry geoext:chemistryRemarks geoext:measuredFormulaSource geoext:mineralogicalAnalysisProtocol
Label Chemistry
Definition Properties that describe the chemical composition of a mineral
geoext:measuredChemistry Property
Label Measured Formula
Definition A concise expression of the chemical composition of a mineral that shows the number of atoms of each element in a molecule, their spatial arrangement, and their linkage to each other.
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • SiO2 (65.76), TiO2 (32.120), Al2O3 (2.21)
  • (Mg0.77Fe0.23)2SiO4
  • An6.4 Ab73.6 Or20
geoext:chemistryRemarks Property
Label Chemistry Remarks
Definition General remarks about the chemical and isotopic composition of a mineral
Material Scope Constituent Part
geoext:measuredFormulaSource Property
Label Measured Formula Source
Definition A list (concatenated and separated) of resources associated with the reported measured chemistry described specifically enough to allow anyone in the future to use the same resources.
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • Novak, G. A., & Gibbs, G. V. (1971). The crystal chemistry of the silicate garnets. American Mineralogist: Journal of Earth and Planetary Materials, 56(5-6), 791-825.
geoext:mineralogicalAnalysisProtocol Property
Label Mineralogical Analysis Protocol
Definition The technique utilized to determine the chemical composition or crystallography of a mineral
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • Wet Chemistry
  • X-ray fluorescence
  • Electron probe microanalysis
  • Scanning electron microscopy with energy-dispersive X-ray spectroscopy
  • X=ray diffraction

Examples

ConstituentPart

geoext:ConstituentPart geoext:materialEntityID geoext:constituentPartID geoext:constituentPartType geoext:constituentPartProportion geoext:constituentPartRole
Label Constituent Part
Definition A physically discernable part of a compound specimen that has a distinct proportion and role relative to the parent object and a discrete determination based on physical and chemical characteristics. All parts of a compound specimen are unified through physical attachment or other unifying criteria.
Usage Note Specimens comprised of and defined by Constituent Parts unified through physical attachment or other unifying criteria are referred to as Compound Specimens
geoext:materialEntityID Property
Label Material Entity ID
Definition Globally unique identifier of the parent dwc:MaterialEntity. Establishes a relationship with an Material Entity record
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • 8fa58e08-08de-4ac1-b69c-1235340b7111
geoext:constituentPartID Property
Label Constituent Part ID
Definition A unique identifier for an instance of a ConstituentPart.
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • 8fa58e08-08de-4ac1-b69c-1235340b7111
geoext:constituentPartType Property
Label Constituent Part Type
Definition Term belonging to an upper-level, generalized classification scheme of geoext:ConstituentPart
Material Scope Constituent Part
Usage Note The formal name assigned to a geoscience collection is traditionally based on the predominant Constituent Part Type of specimens in the collection or the field of science associated with the respective part type (e.g., Mineral Collection or Mineralogy Collection).
Examples
  • Mineral
  • Fossil
  • Rock
geoext:constituentPartProportion Property
Label Proportion
Definition The qualitative or quantitative fraction the geologic specimen is composed of the constituent part
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • 20%
  • minor
  • dominant
geoext:constituentPartRole Property
Label Role
Definition Specifies the relationship between a constituent part and its parent geologic specimen
Material Scope Constituent Part
Usage Note The role defines the relationship between a constituent part and its parent compound object.
Examples
  • matrix
  • groundmass
  • phenocryst
  • xenolith
  • vein

GeologicContext

dwc:GeologicContext geoext:geologicProvince geoext:lithodemicUnit geoext:geologicEvent geoext:mineralSequence geoext:tectonicUnits
Label Geologic Context
Definition Geological information, such as stratigraphy, that qualifies a region or place.
geoext:geologicProvince Property
Label Geologic Province
Definition Extensive named region with a similar geologic history throughout or similar structural, petrographic, or physiographic features in which the dwc:Location occurs
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • Coastal Plain
  • Piedmont
  • Blue Ridge
  • Colorado Plateaus
  • Basin and Range
  • Dalradian Metamorphic Belt
geoext:lithodemicUnit Property
Label Lithodemic Unit
Definition Geologic unit that lacks stratification, primarily comprised of intrusive, deformed, and (or) metamorphosed rock, and characterized by irregularly mixed lithology or highly complicated structural relations.
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • Catalina Core Complex (named unit) igneous complex
  • injection complex
  • metamorphic complex
  • basement complex
geoext:geologicEvent Property
Label Geologic Event
Definition An identifiable event during which one or more geological processes associated with the formation of a geological specimen or act to modify an existing geological specimen.
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • Sevier orogeny
  • Alleghanian orogeny
  • Alpine orogeny
  • Variscan orogeny
  • Waipounamu Erosion surface
  • Vredefort impact
geoext:mineralSequence Property
Label Mineral Sequence
Definition A list of minerals in a specimen ordered in a manner that illustrates the relative timing of mineral formation within a specimen.
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • Sphalerite > Quartz > Pyrite
  • Calcite > Quartz > Sphalerite > Pyrite
geoext:tectonicUnits Property
Label Tectonic Units
Definition The combination of all tectonic unit names for the rock from which the dwc:MaterialEntity was collected.
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • Upper Helvetic, Wildhorn Nappe Complex, Sublage Nappe

Location

dwc:Location geoext:sampledFeature geoext:namedPlace geoext:isTypeLocality
Label Location
Definition A spatial region or named place.
geoext:sampledFeature Property
Label Sampled Feature
Definition The type of physical feature, both naturally-occuring and anthropogenic in origin, sampled during a sampling event (dwc:Event) at a dwc:Location
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • float
  • mine dump
  • mine/quarry pit
  • outcrop
  • erratic boulder
geoext:namedPlace Property
Label Named Place
Definition The full, unabbreviated name of a geographic location not otherwise categorized by a dwc:Location property that is naturally occurring or anthropogenic in origin within a historical, administrative, or cultural context in which dcterms:Location occurs.
Material Scope Specimen
Examples
  • Susanna Mine
  • Craigleith Quarry
  • Red Cloud Mine
geoext:isTypeLocality Property
Label Is Type Locality
Definition Whether or not the dwc:Location is the same locality where the original material used for the formal definition of the mineral species originated.
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • True
  • False

geoext:inclusions Property
Label Inclusions
Definition Short description of any inclusions present within a mineral that includes the phase and physical characteristics
Material Scope Constituent Part
Usage Note Inclusions are defined as small foreign bodies (solid, liquid, or gas) within the physical bounds of a material commonly trapped during formation

  • Star-shaped rutile needles in quartz
  • Needles of tourmaline in quartz (blue quarz)
  • Fluid inclusions (liquid bubble and single crystal) in quartz
geoext:luminescence Property
Label Luminescence Description
Definition The type and nature of light emitted from the mineral upon receiving energy from an external source.
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • Green fluorescence
  • Pink under short wave UV light
geoext:alterationDescription Property
Label Alteration Description
Definition A description of any observed changes in the composition of a mineral brought about by physical or chemical processes related to changes in the physical or chemical environment.
Material Scope Constituent Part
Examples
  • Dolomitization
  • Fenitization
  • Rodingitization
